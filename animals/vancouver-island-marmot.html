Vancouver Island Marmot

Critically Endangered

Estimated remaining: ~200

Habitat

Subalpine meadows on Vancouver Island, British Columbia, at elevations of 900-1,400 meters, preferring south-facing slopes with early snowmelt for burrowing.

Threats

  • Habitat loss from forestry and climate change
  • Predation by wolves, cougars, and golden eagles
  • Small population size and inbreeding
  • Climate change affecting alpine habitats
  • Historical range contraction

Conservation efforts

Captive breeding and reintroduction program since 1997. Habitat restoration and creation of artificial meadows. Predator management in key colonies. Population monitoring and research. Multi-partner conservation initiative.
