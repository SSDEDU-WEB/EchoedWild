Cross River Gorilla

Critically Endangered

Estimated remaining: 200-300

Habitat

Montane and lowland rainforests along the Nigeria-Cameroon border in the Cross River region. Lives at elevations between 1,500-2,900 meters in rugged, mountainous terrain.

Threats

  • Habitat loss for agriculture and logging
  • Hunting for bushmeat
  • Small, fragmented populations
  • Human-wildlife conflict
  • Limited genetic diversity

Conservation efforts

Transboundary conservation initiatives between Nigeria and Cameroon protect key habitats. Community-based conservation programs engage local people in protection. Camera trapping and genetic monitoring help track population health and movements.
